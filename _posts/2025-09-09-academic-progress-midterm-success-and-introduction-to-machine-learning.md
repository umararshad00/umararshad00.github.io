## First Quiz and Initial Pressure

As my university journey continued, I started experiencing the real academic challenges and learning opportunities that come with higher education. One of the early academic assessments we faced was our first quiz conducted by a teacher. Unfortunately, the quiz turned out to be difficult for many students in the class. Almost five students struggled with it because the questions were a bit challenging and most of us had not prepared as thoroughly as we should have. I personally also felt that my preparation was not strong enough at that time, which resulted in a poor performance.

That first quiz changed my attitude toward small assessments. Before that, I treated a quiz like something that could be handled quickly, but the result showed me that university quizzes can test real understanding. The disappointment was uncomfortable, but it was also useful because it exposed a weakness early in the semester. Instead of waiting until midterms or finals to realize that my preparation style was not enough, I learned it at a time when I could still improve.

## Preparing Again with Better Discipline

Recognizing the difficulty of the quiz and the overall class performance, the quiz was conducted again. This time, I made sure to prepare properly and revise the concepts more carefully. When the quiz was conducted again, my performance improved significantly. This experience taught me the importance of consistent preparation and not underestimating any assessment in university.

When the quiz was conducted again, I approached it with a different mindset. I reviewed the concepts more carefully, practiced more examples, and tried to understand the reason behind each answer instead of only memorizing. The improvement gave me confidence, but it also reminded me that confidence should come after preparation, not before it.

## Lesson from Early Assessment

This experience became one of my first personal examples of academic growth. It showed me that poor performance is not the end if I respond to it honestly and work on the weak areas. It also reminded me of the learning discipline emphasized by Dr. Bilal Ahmad in Programming Fundamentals. His approach helped me understand that programming is not only about memorizing syntax, but also about developing a habit of regular practice and logical thinking.

The first quiz experience also became useful for my portfolio writing. If I only wrote about achievements, my digital footprint would not show the complete learning journey. By documenting this moment, I can show that improvement often begins with a mistake, honest reflection, and better preparation.
