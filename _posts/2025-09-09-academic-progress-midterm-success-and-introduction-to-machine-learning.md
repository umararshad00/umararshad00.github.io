As my university journey continued, I started experiencing the real academic challenges that come with higher education. One of the first important lessons came through an early quiz taken by Sir Deepchal. The quiz was difficult for many students, and several of us struggled because we had not prepared as seriously as we should have.

My own performance in that assessment was not strong, and I felt disappointed. However, that moment became a turning point rather than a failure. After recognizing the difficulty of the quiz and the general performance of the class, Sir Deepchal decided to conduct it again. This time, I prepared properly and revised the concepts with more focus.

When the quiz was repeated, my performance improved significantly. That experience taught me a very practical lesson: in university, no assessment should be underestimated. Even a small quiz can reveal the difference between casual reading and serious preparation.

Soon after that, our midterm examinations approached. By then, I had already understood that university studies require regular effort rather than occasional bursts of study. I spent more time revising my course material, especially for Programming Fundamentals, and I approached the exam with better confidence.

As a result, I scored 32 marks out of 40 in Programming Fundamentals, which was a satisfying achievement for me. Usually, the midterm carries 30 marks, but our instructor included some additional questions, so the paper was conducted out of 40. Even with a slightly expanded paper, my preparation allowed me to perform well. That result boosted my confidence and encouraged me to keep working with discipline.

After midterms, our academic attention gradually shifted toward Machine Learning, which introduced us to a completely new and exciting field. We started by learning important Python libraries, especially Pandas, which helped us understand how data can be organized, cleaned, and analyzed.

Later, we moved toward machine learning models, particularly the Decision Tree algorithm. We solved numerical problems by hand, which gave me a clearer understanding of how the logic behind decision trees actually works. These exercises were valuable because they connected mathematics, reasoning, and practical computing.

We also had a quiz related to Pandas and Decision Trees. Most of the questions were based on Pandas, and fortunately I felt more confident this time because I had prepared well. That small success showed me how far I had come from my first weak quiz. This phase of the semester taught me that academic growth is often the result of learning from mistakes, staying consistent, and building confidence one step at a time.
