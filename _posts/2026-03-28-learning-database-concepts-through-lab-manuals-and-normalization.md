## Performing Database Lab Tasks and Understanding Projection

After learning the basic concepts of SQL and database operations, our focus shifted towards practical implementation in the lab. During this period, Dr. Bilal Ahmad guided us through different database exercises and lab activities. Instead of only learning theory, we were encouraged to perform tasks ourselves so that we could understand how database concepts work in real applications.

One of the important concepts we studied was Projection. We learned how projection is used to retrieve specific columns from a database table instead of displaying all available information. Through different examples and exercises, I understood how this concept helps make database queries more efficient and organized. At first, database operations seemed a little confusing because they were different from traditional programming concepts, but regular practice made them easier to understand.

In our lab sessions, we completed several practical tasks related to SQL and database management. After performing each task, we were required to prepare a Lab Manual explaining the objectives, procedures, SQL queries, outputs, and conclusions. Preparing these lab manuals helped me revise the concepts and improve my technical documentation skills. It also taught me how to present technical work in a structured and professional manner.

The combination of theory and practical work made learning more effective. Instead of simply memorizing concepts, I was able to see their practical applications. This approach helped me build confidence in working with databases and strengthened my understanding of how information is managed in real systems.

## Learning Data Integrity, Relationships, and Normalization

In the following week, Dr. Bilal Ahmad introduced several new database concepts that are essential for designing efficient and reliable databases. One of the first topics we discussed was Data Integrity. I learned that data integrity ensures that information stored in a database remains accurate, consistent, and reliable. This concept helped me understand why proper database design is important for maintaining high-quality data.

We also studied Data Relationships, which explain how different tables are connected with each other. Through classroom discussions and examples, I learned how databases use relationships to organize information efficiently. Understanding relationships helped me see how large systems store and connect different types of data without unnecessary duplication.

Another major topic during this phase was Normalization. At first, normalization seemed like a difficult concept because it involved organizing data into different forms. However, after studying examples and practicing exercises, I gradually understood its purpose. We learned about First Normal Form (1NF) and Second Normal Form (2NF) and discussed how normalization helps reduce redundancy and improve database structure.

Normalization showed me how a poorly designed database can create problems such as duplicate information and inconsistencies. By applying normalization techniques, databases become more organized and easier to manage. Learning these concepts helped me understand the importance of proper planning before creating a database system.

## Practical Lab Implementation and Lessons Learned

After learning these concepts in theory, we performed practical implementations in the lab. We applied normalization techniques to sample databases and observed how data structures changed when different normalization rules were applied. This practical work helped me understand the concepts much better than simply reading them from a textbook.

As with previous lab activities, we were required to prepare detailed Lab Manuals documenting our work. These manuals included the problem statement, implementation steps, results, and conclusions. Writing the manuals required extra effort, but it helped me revise the concepts and understand them more deeply.

One thing I appreciate about Dr. Bilal Ahmad's teaching style is his focus on practical learning. Rather than only explaining concepts through lectures, he encourages students to perform tasks themselves. Since Dr. Bilal Ahmad works in Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning, he often highlights the importance of well-structured and high-quality data. Through these discussions, I realized that database concepts such as integrity, relationships, and normalization are also important foundations for data science and machine learning projects.

Looking back, this phase of the semester was very beneficial for my learning. I not only improved my understanding of database systems but also developed better problem-solving and documentation skills. The biggest lesson I learned during this period was that a well-designed database is the foundation of any successful software system.
