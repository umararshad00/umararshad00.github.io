## Understanding Continuous Assessment

In the following week, our proper academic journey began. One of the first major differences I noticed between college and university life was the academic system. In college, students often prepare mainly at the end of the year for final examinations. However, university life is very different because students have to stay prepared throughout the semester. The evaluation system includes quizzes, midterm exams, assignments, lab tasks, and final exams, and all these components contribute to the final GPA. This continuous assessment system requires consistent effort and time management.

This continuous system changed the way I looked at preparation. In college, I could sometimes delay revision and still manage by studying near the exams, but university did not allow that mindset for long. Every week had some academic expectation attached to it. A quiz could come after a small topic, a lab task could test practical understanding, and an assignment could require extra effort outside class time. At first, this felt stressful, but gradually I understood that the system was designed to keep students active throughout the semester.

## Programming Fundamentals with Python

During the early weeks of the semester, we started studying Programming Fundamentals, where we began learning the Python programming language. This course had a slightly different structure because theory classes and lab sessions were conducted separately by different instructors. Our theory classes were guided by Dr. Bilal Ahmad, who explained the fundamental programming concepts and the theoretical aspects of Python. On the other hand, our lab sessions were conducted by our lab teacher, where we practiced coding and implemented the concepts that we learned in theory classes.

Programming Fundamentals was one of the first courses that made me feel like I was entering the real field of Computer Engineering. Python was not only a subject name; it was a new way of thinking. I had to understand how instructions are written, how a computer follows logic, and how small mistakes in syntax can change the output. The theory classes gave me the foundation, while the labs showed me whether I had truly understood the concept or not.

## Lab Quizzes and Early Challenges

After two or three weeks of classes, our quizzes began. The quizzes related to theory were conducted on paper, while the lab quizzes were taken on the computer systems in the laboratory. These quizzes were an important part of the evaluation system and encouraged us to keep revising the topics regularly. However, sometimes we also faced small challenges related to the learning environment. I remember one particular instance when we were about to take a lab quiz, but suddenly the electricity went out due to load shedding. Because of this issue, the quiz had to be postponed and was taken one week later.

Another similar situation occurred during one of our open-ended lab sessions, which was also affected by electricity problems. Since the activity could not be completed at that time, it had to be rescheduled later. Despite these minor difficulties, these experiences taught me an important lesson about adaptability and persistence. Dr. Bilal Ahmad's teaching in Programming Fundamentals also helped me connect programming with future areas like AI and machine learning. His professional background in AI, ML, and deep learning made this foundation feel more meaningful because it showed that every advanced skill begins with basic concepts done properly.
