## Dataset Selection Process

Another important part of this phase was the dataset selection process. Our instructor provided multiple datasets and asked students to choose one based on a "first come, first serve" system. This created a sense of urgency and decision-making among students, as everyone wanted to select a dataset that was both interesting and manageable. I carefully reviewed the available options and selected a dataset that suited my understanding and project requirements.

This experience taught me how to make quick yet effective decisions and understand the importance of choosing the right dataset for a machine learning project. A dataset is not just a file; it decides the direction of the whole project. The selected data affects preprocessing, visualization, model choice, evaluation method, and even the final explanation.

## Why Dataset Choice Matters

Dataset selection also taught me that a project begins before coding. If the dataset does not match the student's level or the project objective, the work becomes harder later. I had to think about whether the columns were understandable, whether the target variable was clear, and whether I could explain the problem properly. This made the selection process more than just choosing an interesting topic.

I also realized that a dataset should be manageable enough for learning but meaningful enough to create a useful project. This balance is important for students because an overly complex dataset can create confusion, while an overly simple dataset may not provide enough learning. The dataset related to used car prices gave me a practical problem that I could understand and explain.

## Data Quality and Real-World Value

Dr. Bilal Ahmad often emphasizes that machine learning should connect with problems that have real value in the world. His interest in AI, ML, and DL model training, especially with precise datasets such as medical datasets, helped me understand that data quality matters deeply. A model trained on weak or careless data cannot be trusted, no matter how advanced the algorithm looks.

Overall, this stage of my learning journey significantly enhanced my practical skills. I not only learned how to select a dataset but also understood why dataset quality affects the entire machine learning process. Writing this reflection on my portfolio shows how my understanding moved from simple coding toward complete project thinking.
