Creating a personal website taught me that design is not only about looks. It is also about structure, clarity, and helping visitors find the right information quickly.

When everything is placed on one page, it can feel crowded. That is why separating the homepage from the blog is such a useful decision. The homepage can stay clean and fixed, while the blog becomes the place for regular posts and updates.

## A clear first impression

The first page should tell people who you are before it tells them what you have written. A short introduction, education details, key skills, and contact access create a much stronger first impression than a long list of posts.

## Good design supports good content

A professional layout makes reading easier. Clean spacing, readable typography, and a simple navigation bar help visitors move through the site with confidence.

## Small projects build confidence

This project reminded me that even a small website can be valuable. It gives you a place to present yourself, practice your skills, and improve over time.
