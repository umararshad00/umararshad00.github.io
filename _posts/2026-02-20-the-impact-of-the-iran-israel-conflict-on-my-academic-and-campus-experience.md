## Shift from Campus to Online Learning

During my university journey, external circumstances also played a significant role in shaping my academic experience. One such situation was the impact of the Iran-Israel conflict, which indirectly affected our daily campus life. Due to rising tensions in the region, there were concerns related to oil supply, which eventually led to fuel shortages and economic pressure. As a result, our university had to make adjustments to ensure the continuity of education. One of the major changes was the shift from physical classes to online learning platforms. Some of our classes were conducted online, and instead of attending lectures on campus, we had to rely on digital tools such as WhatsApp and other communication platforms. This transition required me to quickly adapt to a new mode of learning, where self-discipline and time management became even more important. Although it was different from the traditional classroom environment, it helped me develop flexibility and improve my ability to learn independently.

The sudden shift to online learning showed me how quickly an academic routine can change because of events outside the university. One week students may be preparing to attend physical classes, and the next week they may have to depend on messages, shared files, and online instructions. This was not the same as sitting in a classroom where questions can be asked immediately. Online communication required more attention because missing one message could mean missing an update about class, quiz, or assignment. I had to become more responsible in checking groups and keeping track of academic instructions.

This experience also made me appreciate the value of digital tools. Even though online classes were not always ideal, they allowed learning to continue when physical attendance became difficult. For a Computer Engineering student, this was also a real example of how technology supports education during disruption. It reminded me that digital platforms are not only for convenience; sometimes they become necessary for continuity.

## Commute Challenges and Routine Change

Another significant impact of this situation was on my daily commute and routine. Due to fuel shortages, transportation became both difficult and expensive. Traveling to the university was no longer as convenient as before, which forced me to rethink how I managed my time and attendance. In many cases, I had to depend more on online communication and digital resources to keep up with my studies. This experience taught me how external factors, even those beyond academic control, can influence student life. It also encouraged me to become more organized and proactive in managing my schedule, ensuring that I did not fall behind in my coursework despite these challenges.

The commute issue affected more than travel; it affected energy and planning. When transport becomes uncertain, a student has to think about timing, cost, and reliability before even reaching class. This can create mental pressure because academic work is already demanding. I realized that many students face challenges that are not visible in marksheets. A quiz or assignment may look simple from the outside, but the student's routine behind it may be disturbed by transport, electricity, internet, or family responsibilities.

Because of this, I tried to manage my study routine more carefully. I saved important notes, followed class updates, and tried to complete work whenever I had stable time. This was not always easy, but it helped me stay connected with the semester. I also learned that waiting for perfect conditions is not practical. Sometimes a student has to continue learning even when the environment is imperfect.

## Campus Interaction and Adaptability

In addition to academic disruptions, the conflict also affected the social and interactive aspects of campus life. Many on-campus activities, group discussions, and collaborative sessions were either postponed or shifted to online formats. This change reduced face-to-face interaction with classmates and limited opportunities for teamwork in a physical setting. I realized how important in-person collaboration is for effective learning and communication. Working on projects virtually was different and sometimes less engaging, but it also helped me improve my digital communication and teamwork skills in an online environment.

The loss of normal campus interaction made me understand how much learning happens outside formal lectures. Short discussions with classmates, quick questions after class, and group study moments all help students understand topics better. When those interactions reduce, learning can feel more isolated. Online communication helped, but it did not fully replace the natural flow of physical collaboration. This gave me a deeper appreciation for campus life and the social side of education.

At the same time, virtual teamwork taught me useful skills. I had to explain my ideas more clearly in messages, share resources properly, and coordinate without always meeting in person. These are practical skills because modern academic and professional work often involves remote collaboration. So even though the situation was difficult, it still taught me something valuable about communication and adaptability.

Despite these challenges, this experience became a valuable part of my learning journey. It taught me resilience, adaptability, and the importance of maintaining consistency even in uncertain situations. I learned that while external factors can disrupt normal routines, the ability to adjust and continue progressing is what truly defines a successful student. Overall, this phase not only tested my academic discipline but also strengthened my ability to handle real-life challenges alongside my studies.

This period also showed why documenting student experiences matters. If I only write about smooth academic achievements, my portfolio will not show the full reality of learning. Real student life includes interruptions, pressure, uncertainty, and adjustment. By writing about this phase, I am recording how external events affected my education and how I responded. That makes my digital footprint more honest and complete.

In the larger sense, this experience helped me understand that education is not separate from the world around it. Regional events, economic pressure, fuel issues, and institutional decisions can all shape a student's semester. Learning to continue under such conditions is a skill in itself. I may not remember every detail years later, but this post will remind me that I learned resilience during a challenging academic period.
