## Return to Campus

After the campus lockdown and the vacation period caused by the Iran-Israel conflict, our universities finally reopened, allowing us to return to physical classes. This transition back to campus life was both relieving and challenging at the same time. After spending time away from regular academic activities, it took some effort to readjust to the routine of attending classes, focusing on studies, and managing academic responsibilities. However, this return was not entirely smooth, as we were already approaching an important phase of the semester.

Returning to campus felt positive because physical classes bring a different kind of focus. In the classroom, it is easier to listen, ask questions, and feel connected with the subject. Meeting classmates again also made the academic environment feel active. However, the return came with pressure because the semester schedule had already been disturbed. We were not simply restarting from a relaxed point; we were entering a compressed academic phase where pending work and upcoming exams were close together.

This situation made the reopening both helpful and stressful. On one side, physical classes gave us a chance to reconnect with teachers and clarify concepts. On the other side, the limited time before midterms created urgency. I had to adjust my routine quickly, wake up according to campus timing again, manage travel, and prepare mentally for assessments. This showed me that returning to normal after a disruption is also a challenge.

## Quizzes, Midterms, and Pending Work

Within just one week of reopening, our midterm examinations were scheduled to begin. This created a sense of urgency among students, as we had limited time to revise and prepare. During this week, teachers started conducting pre-midterm quizzes, which are usually taken before exams as part of internal assessment. However, due to time constraints and the disruption caused by the earlier lockdown, only some teachers were able to conduct these quizzes, while others could not. This created an unusual situation where some of our quizzes were completed before the midterms, while the remaining ones were postponed and planned to be taken along with or after the midterm exams.

This was one of the busiest academic periods because everything seemed to arrive at once. Quizzes, revisions, pending topics, and midterm preparation were all competing for attention. I realized that when a schedule is disrupted, work does not disappear; it often comes back in a compressed form. This is why regular preparation matters. If I had revised more consistently during the uncertain period, the reopening week would have felt easier. This was another hard but useful lesson.

This situation increased the academic pressure significantly. Students were not only required to prepare for their midterm exams but also had to stay ready for pending quizzes at the same time. Managing both together was quite challenging, as each subject required proper attention, revision, and practice. It became difficult to balance everything within a limited time frame, especially after the disruption in our academic schedule. Despite these difficulties, I tried to stay organized and focused, making study plans to cover both quizzes and exams effectively.

Making a study plan became necessary rather than optional. I had to decide which subject needed immediate revision, which quiz was expected first, and which topics were weak. Without a plan, the pressure could easily become confusing. I also learned that planning does not mean everything becomes easy; it simply gives direction. Even if I could not follow the plan perfectly, it helped me avoid wasting time deciding what to study next.

This phase reminded me of the final week pressure from the previous semester, where late preparation made tasks feel heavier. The same lesson appeared again in a new form. University life repeatedly teaches that consistency is better than emergency preparation. When I delay work, I may still complete it, but the stress increases. When I prepare earlier, I can handle sudden changes with more confidence.

## Hybrid Schedule and Lessons Learned

Another major change during this period was the new academic schedule introduced by the government. According to this policy, physical classes were limited to four days a week (Monday to Thursday), while Friday classes were shifted to online mode. This hybrid system created a new learning environment where students had to manage both on-campus and online classes simultaneously. Adjusting to this system required flexibility, as we had to switch between physical attendance and online learning regularly.

The hybrid schedule was practical in some ways but also required discipline. From Monday to Thursday, I had to follow the physical campus routine, and on Friday I had to shift back to online learning. This change in mode could easily disturb focus if not managed properly. Physical classes required travel and direct participation, while online classes required checking messages, links, and timing carefully. I had to treat both modes seriously because missing an online class could affect preparation just like missing a physical class.

This hybrid system also showed how education is becoming more flexible. A student now needs to be comfortable in both physical and digital environments. For me, this connected with the broader idea of building a digital academic presence. If classes, resources, communication, and portfolios are becoming more digital, then maintaining a GitHub portfolio is a useful practice. It helps me become more comfortable presenting my learning online.

Although these circumstances created several challenges, they also taught me important lessons about time management, adaptability, and consistency. I learned how to cope with academic pressure, manage multiple tasks at once, and stay committed to my studies despite uncertainties. This phase of my university journey highlighted the importance of being prepared for unexpected changes and maintaining a strong work ethic even in difficult situations. Overall, it was a demanding yet valuable experience that contributed to my personal and academic growth.

Looking back, this period was not easy, but it made me more aware of my responsibilities as a student. I learned that academic success depends not only on intelligence but also on routine, planning, and emotional control under pressure. When campus reopened, I had to move quickly from uncertainty to action. That transition tested me, but it also helped me grow.

Writing about this experience completes an important part of my semester story. It connects the disruption caused by external events with the practical challenge of returning to studies. It also shows that my portfolio is not only a place for polished achievements. It is a record of how I handled pressure, made adjustments, and kept moving forward as a Computer Engineering student.
