## Returning to Classes After the Holiday Break

After the holiday period, our classes resumed, and we returned to our regular academic routine. Like many students, I was excited to continue learning and move forward with the semester. However, the transition back to university was not completely smooth. Due to the holidays and interruptions in the academic schedule, some classes could not be conducted as effectively as originally planned. This affected the pace of learning during the first few days after reopening. As a result, some topics had to be covered more quickly, and both students and teachers had to adjust to the updated schedule.

Despite these challenges, returning to campus brought back the energy and motivation that had been missing during the break. Being physically present in classrooms again helped improve interaction with teachers and classmates. It also reminded me of the importance of maintaining consistency in my studies. Even a short academic break can disrupt learning momentum, making it necessary to put in extra effort to get back on track. This experience taught me that staying connected with academic work, even during holidays, can make the transition back to classes much easier.

As the semester continued, we began exploring a new and exciting subject: Database Systems. Unlike some of the courses I had studied previously, this subject focused on how information is stored, organized, and managed efficiently. Since almost every modern application depends on databases, I was eager to learn how these systems work behind the scenes.

## Learning the Foundations of Database Systems

The introduction to Database Systems opened a completely new area of learning for me. Before this course, I had primarily focused on programming concepts through Python and Programming Fundamentals. Now, I was beginning to understand where the data used by applications is actually stored and how it is managed. This connection between programming and databases made the subject even more interesting.

Our Database Systems course was taught by Dr. Bilal Ahmad, who had also taught us Programming Fundamentals. Having already learned under his guidance, I was familiar with his teaching style and his emphasis on practical learning. One thing I appreciate about Dr. Bilal Ahmad is that he always tries to connect classroom concepts with real-world applications. As someone who works extensively in Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning research, he often highlights the importance of high-quality and well-organized data. Through his examples, I began to understand that databases are not just storage systems; they are the foundation of modern software applications and intelligent systems.

During the initial lectures, we learned the basic concepts of databases and why they are essential in the digital world. We discussed how organizations use databases to store large amounts of information and how efficient database management helps improve performance, security, and reliability. These discussions helped me realize that databases play a critical role in almost every industry, from healthcare and education to finance and technology.

The subject also encouraged me to think beyond coding. While programming focuses on building solutions, databases ensure that the information required by those solutions is available, accurate, and organized. Understanding this relationship helped me see the bigger picture of software development and computer engineering.

## Understanding SQL Operations: Selection, Projection, and Rename

As we progressed further, we were introduced to SQL (Structured Query Language), which is the standard language used to interact with databases. SQL was different from the programming languages I had learned before because its primary purpose is to retrieve, manage, and manipulate data. Initially, it seemed unfamiliar, but after studying examples and practicing queries, I started appreciating its simplicity and power.

One of the first concepts we learned was Selection. This operation allows users to retrieve specific records from a database based on particular conditions. Through various examples, I learned how selection helps filter data and display only the information that is relevant to a specific requirement. This concept demonstrated how databases can efficiently handle large amounts of information while providing precise results.

Another important concept we studied was Projection. Projection focuses on retrieving specific columns or attributes from a database table rather than displaying all available information. Learning projection helped me understand the importance of efficiency in database operations. Instead of retrieving unnecessary data, users can focus only on the fields they need. This concept showed me how databases optimize information retrieval and improve performance.

We also learned about the Rename operation, which allows attributes or database objects to be given new names for better readability and understanding. Although it appeared to be a simple concept, I quickly realized its importance in database design. Meaningful names make databases easier to understand, especially when working on larger projects involving multiple tables and relationships.

Looking back, this phase of the semester played an important role in expanding my technical knowledge. Learning Selection, Projection, and Rename gave me my first practical experience with database querying and helped me build a strong foundation for more advanced database topics that would follow later in the semester.
