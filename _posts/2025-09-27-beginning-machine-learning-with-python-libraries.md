## First Steps into Machine Learning

After completing the midterm exams, our academic focus gradually shifted toward Machine Learning, which introduced us to a completely new and exciting area of study. In the beginning, we started learning about important Python libraries used for data analysis. One of the first libraries we studied was Pandas, which is widely used for handling and analyzing datasets. Learning Pandas helped me understand how data can be organized, cleaned, and prepared for analysis.

Machine Learning felt exciting because it showed a bigger purpose behind the programming skills we had been building. Pandas helped me see data as something structured and workable, not just numbers in a file. When I loaded or imagined a dataset, I started thinking about rows, columns, missing values, and useful features.

## Decision Trees and Visualization

After becoming familiar with basic libraries, we moved toward understanding machine learning models, particularly the Decision Tree algorithm. During this stage, we solved several numerical problems by hand, which helped us understand the mathematical and logical concepts behind decision trees. These exercises gave me valuable insight into how machine learning algorithms actually work.

Later, we also learned about data visualization and coding techniques, which allowed us to represent data in graphical forms for better understanding and analysis. Around the same time, we had a quiz related to Pandas and Decision Trees. In that quiz, most of the questions were based on Pandas, and fortunately, I felt confident about my answers because I had prepared well.

## Project Title and Dataset Approval

Eventually, we reached the project phase of the course. Our instructor assigned projects in groups of two students and provided multiple datasets from which we had to choose one. My group selected a dataset related to used car prices. At first, when I submitted my project title, it was rejected because it did not fully meet the required conditions and was submitted late. However, instead of giving up, I revised the title and worked on improving it.

When I submitted the improved version, it was finally accepted. This process taught me that persistence and continuous improvement are essential parts of academic growth. It also matched the practical mindset encouraged by Dr. Bilal Ahmad, who often pushes students to think about data problems that have real value in the world.
