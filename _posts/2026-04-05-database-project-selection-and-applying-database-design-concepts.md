## Forming Project Groups and Selecting a Research Dataset

As the semester continued, we moved from individual lab activities to a more comprehensive database project. For this project, the class was divided into groups of two students. This was an exciting step because it allowed us to apply everything we had learned in a real-world database project. Working in a team also helped us improve our collaboration and communication skills while solving technical problems together.

After forming the groups, Dr. Bilal Ahmad asked each team to select a dataset for their database project. Every group was encouraged to choose a dataset based on their interests and research goals. This process required us to explore different datasets and think carefully about which one would be suitable for database design and implementation.

My partner and I selected a Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) Dataset from the UCI Machine Learning Repository. We chose this dataset because it was related to the medical field and contained structured information that could be used for database design and analysis. Medical datasets are generally collected with a high level of accuracy and detail, which makes them valuable for research and learning purposes. After reviewing our dataset, it was approved for the project, allowing us to move forward with the implementation phase.

## Implementing the Dataset in MySQL Workbench

In the following week, we began working on the practical implementation of our selected dataset. Using MySQL Workbench, we started creating the database structure and importing data into the system.

This was one of the most practical learning experiences of the semester because we were no longer working with small classroom examples. Instead, we were handling a real dataset and applying database concepts directly. We used various SQL queries to create tables, insert records, and organize the dataset within the database environment.

During this phase, I became more comfortable with SQL commands and database management techniques. Writing queries repeatedly helped me understand how databases store and retrieve information efficiently. I also learned how important it is to carefully organize data before using it in any project.

One thing I appreciated about Dr. Bilal Ahmad's teaching approach was his focus on practical implementation. Rather than limiting the project to theory, he encouraged students to work with actual datasets and build complete database solutions. This approach helped me understand how database systems are used in real-world applications, especially in research and data-driven fields.

## Designing ER Diagrams and Applying Normalization

After successfully importing and organizing the dataset, the next step was database design. We created an Entity Relationship (ER) Diagram, which visually represented the structure of our database and the relationships between different entities.

Designing the ER diagram helped me understand how information is connected within a database system. It also showed the importance of planning a database before implementation. A well-designed database structure makes data management easier and reduces future complications.

Once the ER diagram was completed, we applied Normalization techniques to improve the quality of the database design. We studied and implemented First Normal Form (1NF), Second Normal Form (2NF), and Third Normal Form (3NF). Applying these normalization forms helped us remove unnecessary data duplication and organize information more efficiently.

By the end of this phase, our project had evolved from a simple dataset into a properly structured database system. Most importantly, I realized that database development is not just about storing information. It is about designing systems that are organized, efficient, and reliable.
