## Preparing for Midterm Exams

After the early quiz experience, our midterm examinations approached. By this time, I had already understood that university studies require regular effort and preparation. Therefore, I focused more seriously on my studies and spent a considerable amount of time revising the course material. The earlier quiz had already taught me that a weak preparation habit can quickly affect performance, so I tried to approach the midterm phase with a more responsible mindset.

This preparation period was different from my earlier study routine because I was not only reading topics at the last moment. I tried to revise concepts repeatedly, practice examples, and identify weak areas before the exam day. This helped me understand that preparation is not only about spending many hours with books. It is also about using that time with direction.

## Programming Fundamentals Result

In the Programming Fundamentals course, I prepared thoroughly for the exam. As a result of this preparation, I was able to achieve 32 marks out of 40, which was a satisfying outcome for me. Normally, the midterm exam carries 30 marks, but our instructor decided to conduct the exam out of 40 marks because some additional questions were included from the syllabus. Even though the exam had slightly more questions than usual, my preparation helped me perform well.

Scoring 32 out of 40 was important for me because it proved that disciplined preparation can directly affect performance. I did not see it only as marks; I saw it as feedback on my changed study routine. The paper being out of 40 instead of the usual 30 also made the exam feel longer and more demanding, but because I had revised properly, I was able to manage it.

## Confidence Through Consistency

This result boosted my confidence and motivated me to continue working hard in the rest of the semester. It also showed me that university confidence is not built by luck. It is built through revision, practice, and learning from earlier mistakes. Dr. Bilal Ahmad's teaching style in Programming Fundamentals helped me understand that concepts become stronger when they are practiced regularly rather than memorized only for exams.

The midterm phase also helped me understand the value of writing about my experience afterward. By recording the full journey on my portfolio blog, I can see the connection between a weak quiz, better preparation, and improved midterm performance. This continuity is exactly why a GitHub portfolio is useful for students. It does not only show final projects; it shows the process of becoming better.
